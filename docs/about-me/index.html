HELLO!

My name is Arthur (they/them); I use ArthurArthur as an alias to remove myself from societal structures of naming. Though I have a legal name, I see myself as Arthur and nothing else.

My work is a projector of myself and my body. They allow me to process myself and my needs. I use plastics and other stimulating materials as a catalyst for further growth. It’s important to me and my practice to talk about what I have experienced and how it affects my space. Queerness is infinite.

My practice is multimedia and experimental.

My projects grow into performances, sound pieces, woven objects, relief prints, ceramic objects, and books. I want to rewrite the unwritten rules. I want to question who I am and where I have a space. The narratives I am a part of help me feel more myself and in control.

It’s critical for me to collaborate with other artists. The people who surround me, nurture me and shape me. They are as crucial to my art practice as I am.

Currently, I am a student exploring visual design, sculpture, fibers, and performance at The School of the Art Institute of Chicago.
