YOU MADE ME TO LAST BUT YOU DISPOSE OF ME

From start to finish the process of this work was intense.

The material of plastic as a surface to print on was utterly new to me and was interesting to work with. I’ve done a lot of work with plastic, but I’ve never printed with it.

My priority was to make the drying process as smooth as possible. Because I printed on a material that was not absorbent, I needed to make sure I allowed as much time as possible for the ink to dry. Each page took a little over a week to dry thoroughly, and even then, there needed to be leeway if I printed too thick of a layer.

The pages themselves are made of two plastic bags sealed together with heat. I chose a clothing iron as my tool for this project. The direct heat allows the plastic to form together and create insane textures. Each page was assembled and pressed by me.

Mixing ink was a relieving part of this process. I didn’t have a single problem mixing colors or applying ink to the letterpress rollers. I was thankful I could rely on my inks and not have to worry about them.

Setting the type for this project was so exciting. I was finally able to use some of the wood types that were available to me! Overall I think of myself as a pretty skilled former of type and its spacing, but the plastic as my “paper” made things complicated. Because of the warping of each page, the printed type had distortions that made the spacing look incorrect. During this process, there was a constant reconsideration of elements with my type and its word spacing. I was forced to pay even more attention to the legibility and aesthetics of each word and each phrase as a composition. This was an action that I grew to love. Having your hand in every decision made me feel even more proud of the outcome.

The poem displayed within this book was challenging to write and iterate. At first, it was going to be strictly a third-person perspective of telling the reader their thoughts and feelings. The more I was writing and editing; I found that making a first-person experience the central perspective of this poem would capture the minds of more people. I saw myself becoming a part of the poem. Becoming a part of this conversation was difficult to navigate, especially when I was trying to tiptoe around a conversation during the chat.

Putting this book together was something that I thought about the most. I had ideas of binding with a handwoven plastic rope, but experiencing making each page made me feel about bonding the pages together with heat.
