REALITY

This project encompasses the idea of gender strictly as a performance. If gender was only a performance, who would it function, and what would it look like?

A performance of my body. I chose to use my body as my canvas for this performance so that I would not be able to hide. I am always visible. In this performance, I undo and take off all elements of the ensemble that is on my body. The washing machine and shower are both places of cleaning and "purifying" this performance.

I take off and reveal to another performance, which is a collared shirt and dress pants. I think of all clothing as performance in one way or another.

In the space of the laundry room, I quickly remove my ensemble and shove it into a washing machine. I think the cycle of washing clothing is a performance in itself.

In the scene within the shower, I try to rub off all the makeup I have on. It's an uncomfortable transition to look at. This is another layer of removing this fabricated performance to understand how my identity interacts with material objects.

I want to thank my friend and awe-inspiring artist/drag performer Soi Sauce for doing my makeup. And of course my best friend, artist, printmaker, and sculpter, Sarah Urban for helping me document this project.

2021, performance, 2:56, clear plastic grocery bags, inflated suit, faux leather heels, makeup
